What is Escrow?

An escrow is a financial arrangement where a trusted third party holds funds on behalf of two parties involved in a transaction. The funds are released only when both parties fulfill their agreed-upon conditions.

Think of escrow as a secure vault that holds your money until everyone does what they promised.

How Does Escrow Work?

The escrow process follows a simple flow that protects both buyers and sellers:

  • Agreement: Buyer and seller agree on terms
  • Deposit: Buyer deposits funds into the escrow account
  • Fulfillment: Seller delivers the product or service
  • Verification: Buyer verifies receipt and confirms
  • Release: Funds are released to the seller

Why is Escrow Important in Online Transactions?

Online transactions carry inherent risks — you're dealing with strangers, and traditional payment methods offer little recourse if something goes wrong. Escrow eliminates these risks by creating a trusted intermediary.
